Why BundleGuard can't do that to you

Every app above works the same way: a background job that writes stock levels to your store. When it breaks — bad sync, race condition, botched update — your inventory is the casualty. BundleGuard is built differently.

Shopify keeps the count

Bundles are created through Shopify's native bundle engine. Your bundle availability is derived by Shopify itself from component stock. BundleGuard holds zero write access to your inventory.

The failure class is removed, not patched.

Everything the native app won't do

Automatic price sync when component prices change. Bulk create and edit. Mix-and-match bundles. Clean component breakdown on every order for your 3PL.

Power, without the write access.

Location visibility

Shopify bundles track overall stock, not per-location — so BundleGuard warns you when a bundle is exposed to an empty location. Read-only, by principle.

We alert. We never touch.

Honest limitations (so you don't find out later)

  • Subscription / selling-plan bundles aren't supported — a Shopify platform limitation, not a roadmap item. If you need those, BundleGuard isn't for you.
  • Requires Shopify's upgraded checkout (stores still on checkout.liquid customizations are ineligible for native bundles).
  • Fixed bundles: up to 30 components, 3 options — Shopify's limits.
